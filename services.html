What a proper regas actually involves

We connect a full diagnostic machine to your car's air con system, check exactly how much refrigerant is left, then carry out the service properly: a deep vacuum, a leak test under pressure, and a refill to the exact spec for your vehicle.

1. Diagnostic Check

We connect a diagnostic machine and check exactly how much refrigerant is in your system, and whether it needs a service.

2. Deep Vacuum

All the moisture and old PAG oil is vacuumed out of the compressor and system, not just topped up over what's already there.

3. Leak Test & Refill

The system is pressure-tested for leaks, then refilled with the exact refrigerant and amount your vehicle needs.

Why the vacuum step matters

Skipping the vacuum and just topping up the gas is the equivalent of having your car serviced and putting the old oil back in. The moisture and old oil are still in there, doing damage, no matter how much fresh gas goes on top.

Here in Spain, where temperatures are higher, this matters even more. A quick top-up often pushes mostly air into the system rather than gas, leaving it over-pressured and not working properly. A full deep vacuum is the only way to get that crisp, cold air back, not just "a bit cooler."

Anti-bacterial treatment

If your air con smells musty when you switch it on, that's bacteria and mould built up in the ducts, not the gas. Our anti-bacterial treatment clears it out, so what you're breathing in is clean air, not whatever's been sitting in the system since last winter.
