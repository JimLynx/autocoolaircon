Common Air Con Questions

What refrigerant does my car need?

It depends on the age of your vehicle. Cars built before around 2015 almost always use R134a. Cars from 2015 onwards mostly use R1234yf, a newer gas brought in for environmental reasons.

The easiest way to check is the A/C nameplate under the bonnet, it'll state the refrigerant type directly. If you can't find it or aren't sure, send us a photo on WhatsApp and we'll confirm before we arrive.

Why does air con need regassing every year or two?

Refrigerant escapes slowly through seals and hoses even when nothing's wrong. It's a gradual, expected loss, not a fault. A car can lose a meaningful chunk of its gas over twelve months, which is why air con that felt fine last summer can feel weak this summer without any single part failing.

My air con isn't cold anymore, is that always low gas?

Usually, yes, but not always. Low refrigerant is the most common cause. Less commonly it's a failing compressor, a blocked condenser, or an electrical fault in the system. Our diagnostic check tells us which one it is before we touch anything, so you're not paying to guess.

Why does my air con smell musty when I turn it on?

That's bacteria and mould that's built up in the ducts and evaporator, not a problem with the gas itself. It's common in humid climates and happens gradually over time. Our anti-bacterial treatment clears it out.

Can I just top up the gas myself with a can from the shop?

You can, and it might make things feel cooler for a while. But a top-up doesn't remove the moisture and old oil already sitting in the system, and it can leave the system over-pressured rather than properly balanced. It's a temporary fix that can mask a bigger issue, particularly in the kind of heat we get here.

How do I know if I have a leak?

Refrigerant leaks are hard to spot on your own, unlike an oil leak there's no puddle to find. The system just runs low faster than it should. If your air con needed regassing again within a year of the last one, that's usually the sign. Our pressure test during a service picks this up.

Yes. We bring the same diagnostic and regas equipment a garage would use, we just bring it to you instead. Home, workplace, wherever suits, seven days a week, no booking a garage slot or being without your car for the day.
